Interpretation mistakes made in The Chronicle of Michael the Great

  1. Article
  2. Interpretation mistakes was made in The Chronicle of Michael the Great.

When Theologian Jean Baptist Chabot released the first edition of his interpretation of The Chronicle of Michael the Great, he failed to add a comma and left out "Oromoye", meaning "Arameans" in the sentence.


The error result went viral online for years and confused many Arameans when the body text translated into "Othuroye d-henun Suryoye", meaning "Assyrians are Syriacs".


In the first edition, it is clear that the heading says "Shmohe d-'ame d-ith l-hun sefro d-men bnayo d-Shim", meaning "Semitic people descendants of Shem", but no mentions of "Aram" being one of Shem's sons in the subheading, however reading further on page 11, it says: "Bnay Shim: Othuroye, Kaldoye, Ludoye, Oromoye d-henun Suryoye, Ebroye, Forsoye", meaning "Children of Shem: Assyrians, Chaldeans, Lydians, Arameans that is Syriacs, Hebrews, and Persians."
