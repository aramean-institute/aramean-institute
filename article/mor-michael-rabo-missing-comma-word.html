Interpretation mistakes made in The Chronicle of Michael the Great

  1. Article
  2. Interpretation mistakes was made in The Chronicle of Michael the Great.

Theologian Jean-Baptiste Chabot's first edition of his interpretation of the Chronicle of Michael the Great[1] lacked a comma and left out "Arameans" in the sentence when he didn't correct the known errors in the Edessan-Aleppo codex manuscript.


During the copying process, the Aramaic-Syriac manuscript of the Edessan-Aleppo codex was corrupted. Instead of correcting the errors in his work, Chabot passed them on, and the sentence became "Othuroye d-henun Suryoye", which means "Assyrians are Syriacs.". An untrue statement.


As it appears in the first edition, the heading says "Shmohe d-'ame d-ith l-hun sefro d-men bnayo d-Shim", meaning "Semitic people descendants of Shem", but no mentions of "Aram" being one of Shem's sons in the subheading, however reading further on page 11, it says: "Bnay Shim: Othuroye, Kaldoye, Ludoye, Oromoye d-henun Suryoye, Ebroye, Forsoye", meaning "Children of Shem: Assyrians, Chaldeans, Lydians, Arameans that is Syriacs, Hebrews, and Persians."


The error resulted went viral online for years and confused many Arameans around the world.


Thanks to Fr. Dayroyo Isa Aygür, Mushe Görgün, Steffanos Budak, Iskender Demirel, Eliyo Seven Beth Qemez, and Gabriel Yalgin, the error was corrected in a new edition of the Chronicle (Ktābā d-Maktbānút zabne)[2] that was published 2006.
