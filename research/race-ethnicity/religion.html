The ancient Arameans were polytheists who believed and worshipped many deities such as Rimmon, Shamash, Nergal, and Atar, along with other Middle Eastern deities.


It's believed that the first known Aramean who converted to Judaism was Naaman the Syrian after being healed by the God of Israel through Prophet Elisha in the Old Testament.


In the first century, the Arameans and the Hebrews in Israel began to adopt Christianity as the fame of Jesus spread into Aram and Aram-Naharaim (Aramean homeland).
